Common Bookkeeping Mistakes (and how to fix them)

Many small businesses make the same bookkeeping mistakes that are easy to avoid. Addressing them early keeps your books accurate and your tax prep simple.

Mistake 1: Mixing personal and business transactions

Keep separate accounts — it saves time and prevents errors during reconciliation and tax filing.

Mistake 2: Not reconciling regularly

Monthly reconciliation catches errors, duplicate transactions, and unauthorized activity.

Mistake 3: Inconsistent categorization

Use consistent categories to make reporting meaningful and avoid misclassification.

Mistake 4: Forgetting to track sales tax and payroll liabilities

Set reminders and use the reporting features in your bookkeeping software to track liabilities automatically.

Pro tip: A short periodic review with a bookkeeper can prevent small problems from becoming big ones.
